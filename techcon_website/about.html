TechCon began in 2014 as a small community meetup created by passionate developers, designers, and technology enthusiasts who wanted a space to learn, share ideas, and connect. What started with fewer than 50 attendees in a local co-working hub quickly grew into one of the region’s most anticipated annual technology gatherings.

Over the years, TechCon expanded its focus from basic software discussions to cutting-edge innovations—cloud computing, cybersecurity, AI, blockchain, robotics, data science, and more. Each edition introduced hands-on workshops, expert-led sessions, startup showcases, and networking opportunities aimed at empowering both beginners and industry professionals.

Today, TechCon Conference 2024 stands as a decade-strong platform that continues to inspire innovation, foster collaboration, and shape the future of technology across Africa and beyond. Each year builds on the same vision that started it all: a community where knowledge is shared, ideas become solutions, and technology drives positive change.

The mission of TechCon Conference 2024 is to create a dynamic, inclusive, and collaborative platform where innovators, developers, entrepreneurs, and technology enthusiasts can learn, share ideas, and build solutions that shape the future. We aim to empower individuals with knowledge, inspire creativity through cutting-edge insights, and foster meaningful connections that drive technological growth across Africa and the world.

1. Dr. Amara Adebayo:AI Research Scientist, DeepIntel Labs

Biography
1. Dr. Amara Adebayo is a Nigerian-born artificial intelligence researcher with over 12 years of experience in machine learning, natural language processing, and ethical AI design. She holds a PhD in Computer Science from the University of Cape Town and has published groundbreaking work on bias detection in AI systems.
Contribution to the Tech Industry
Dr. Amara led the development of an open-source AI fairness toolkit now used by startups and institutions across Africa to improve algorithm transparency. Her advocacy for responsible AI has influenced policy discussions across multiple African tech councils.

2. Daniel Mensah:Founder & CTO, CloudWave Systems

Biography
Daniel Mensah is a Ghanaian cloud architect and entrepreneur who founded CloudWave Systems, a company specializing in affordable cloud infrastructure services for emerging markets. With a background in distributed systems engineering, he has worked with AWS, Google Cloud, and several African fintech companies.
Contribution to the Tech Industry
Daniel pioneered a low-cost cloud hosting model designed for African startups, reducing deployment costs by over 40%. His platform has helped more than 2,000 small businesses scale securely and efficiently without relying on expensive global providers.

3. Sarah Oti:Cybersecurity Specialist, SecureNet Africa

Biography
Sarah Oti is a cybersecurity expert with 15 years of experience in penetration testing, digital forensics, and network defense. She has trained over 5,000 African youths in ethical hacking and cyber hygiene through community-led programs and online academies.
Contribution to the Tech Industry
Sarah designed one of the first African-focused cybersecurity awareness frameworks used in schools and SMEs. Her work has significantly strengthened security practices across the continent, especially among small businesses vulnerable to cyber threats.
